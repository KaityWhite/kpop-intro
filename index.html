K-pop Basics

What is K-pop?

Many people consider K-pop to be just another genre of music. However, it is much more than that. Generally speaking, K-pop is any pop music that has origins in South Korea. For the most part, that means the production company and the singers themselves are either Korean themselves or have just lived in Korea before their debut. K-pop acually encompasses a variety of genres, including pop, hip-hop, classical, EDM, rock, and many others. K-pop is also more than just music. Most, if not all, K-pop artists not only sing, but also dance and rap. K-pop artists are also expected to be exceptionally attractive.

What makes K-pop special?

Some of what makes K-pop stand out from other genres has to do with how the artists are trained and the strict rules they have to follow. As I mentioned earlier, K-pop artists aren't just singers. They're also highly skilled dancers and rappers. They have it all. K-pop idols are the complete package and extremely skilled entertainers.

Is K-pop acutally popular?

K-pop doesn't just have fans in Korea. It has become a world-wide phenomenon. You may have heard of "Gangnam Style" by the hugely popular idol, Psy. That was one of the first major K-pop hits in the US. You may have also heard of BTS. They are the first K-pop group to hit #1 on the Billboard Top 100. K-pop is enjoyed by millions of people across the world, even despite the language bariers. So yeah, it's pretty popular.
